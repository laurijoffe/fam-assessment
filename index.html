The Unrealistic Deadline

Scenario 4 of 8
The Trust Breach

There is no correct answer.
A

D
Raise it with leadership — this is the kind of behavior that damages organizational trust at a systemic level

Path A gives you more autonomy, a bigger scope, and a genuine chance to build something — but higher risk of failure and less organizational support. Path B is high-visibility, well-resourced, and virtually guaranteed to succeed — but you will be executing someone else's vision with limited room to shape it.
Which response is closest to what you would actually do?
There is no correct answer.
A
Path A — the building opportunity, even with the risk
B
Path B — execution and visibility, done well, is its own form of leadership
C

D

Scenario 6 of 8
The Bad Quarter
Results for the quarter are significantly below plan. Your team executed well — the miss was primarily driven by market conditions outside anyone's control. You are preparing the quarterly update for ownership. Your CEO wants to lead with market context and minimize the performance narrative. You disagree with that framing.
Which response is closest to what you would actually do?
There is no correct answer.
A
Present the data cleanly and lead with performance — ownership can read market context themselves
B
Lead with market context as the CEO wants, but ensure the performance data is clearly presented and not obscured
C
Have a direct conversation with the CEO before the meeting — push for a more balanced framing
D
Let the CEO lead the narrative; add the performance context yourself in the Q&A
Scenario 7 of 8
The Competing Colleague
A peer at your level is clearly positioning for the same role you want. They are politically savvy, well-liked, and good at what they do. You believe you are more qualified for the role — but they have more visibility with the decision-maker. You have six months before the decision.
Which response is closest to what you would actually do?
There is no correct answer.
A
Focus entirely on doing exceptional work and trust that results speak for themselves
B
Invest in building your relationship with the decision-maker — visibility matters as much as performance
C
Find ways to collaborate with the colleague directly — turn a competitor into an ally
D
Have an honest conversation with the decision-maker about your interest in the role and your qualifications
Scenario 8 of 8
The Inherited Crisis
You are three months into a new leadership role. You have discovered a significant operational problem that predates your arrival — one that ownership is not aware of and that will require significant resources and difficult decisions to fix. Your predecessor may have known about it.
Which response is closest to what you would actually do?
There is no correct answer.
A
Surface it to ownership immediately, even before you have a full picture or a solution
B
Spend two more weeks fully understanding the problem before bringing it forward
C
Bring it forward simultaneously with a concrete remediation plan
D
Fix as much as you can quietly before surfacing it — minimize the blast radius
